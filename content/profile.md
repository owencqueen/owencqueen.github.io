# Designing foundation models that scientists can trust.

Hello! I'm Owen Queen, a computer science Ph.D. student and Knight-Hennessy Scholar at Stanford University, advised by [James Zou](https://www.james-zou.com/). I study how AI can accelerate scientific discovery by building models that carry context across biomedical data, reason under distribution shifts, and communicate their thinking to researchers.

My current work centers on foundation models for biomedicine, emergent reasoning, interpretability, and scientist-in-the-loop interfaces. Previously, I was a research associate with [Marinka Zitnik](https://zitniklab.hms.harvard.edu/) at Harvard Medical School, contributing to GraphXAI, TimeX, and ProCyon.
