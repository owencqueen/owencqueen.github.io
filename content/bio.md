_From Tennessee to Stanford_

# Grounded in math, sharpened by collaborative science.

I grew up in Watertown, Tennessee and graduated from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville with degrees in computer science and mathematics plus a minor in statistics. I worked closely with mentors including Christopher Strickland, Vasileios Maroulas, Kostas Vogiatzis, and Scott Emrich, and I was honored to receive the Barry Goldwater Scholarship in 2021.

Before Stanford, I spent two years at Harvard Medical School in Marinka Zitnik's lab. There, I helped build systems such as GraphXAI, TimeX, and ProCyon, collaborating with scientists and clinicians on drug discovery, explainability, and multimodal reasoning.
